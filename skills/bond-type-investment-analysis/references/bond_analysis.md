# Bond Analysis and Investment Framework

## 1. Spread Analysis (利差分析)

### Term Spread (期限利差)
- **Definition**: Spread between long-term and short-term bonds (usually 10-year minus 1-year CDB bonds or Treasury bonds).
- **Implication**: Reflects yield curve steepness. Long-end is driven by economic fundamentals; short-end by monetary policy/liquidity.
- **Trading Signal**: If the term spread is too narrow, the long-end yield's downward space is constrained unless the short-end falls. A widening spread often indicates an opportunity to lengthen duration if monetary easing is expected.

### Credit Spread (信用利差)
- **Definition**: Yield difference between credit bonds and rate bonds (e.g., 3-year AA MTN minus 3-year CDB bond).
- **Implication**: Compensates for default risk (driven by economic fundamentals) and liquidity risk (driven by monetary policy).
- **Cycle**: In monetary tightening, rate bonds react first (spread narrows temporarily), then credit bonds spike (spread widens). In economic recovery, default risk drops, leading to spread narrowing.

### Industry & Rating Spread (行业利差与等级利差)
- **Industry Spread**: Compare industry fundamentals against their spreads. If fundamentals improve but spreads remain high, it's a buying opportunity (e.g., Coal/Steel during supply-side reform). If fundamentals deteriorate but spreads are low, sell.
- **Rating Spread**: Spread between high and low-grade credit bonds. Widens during liquidity shocks or economic downturns due to risk aversion; narrows during "asset scarcity" or monetary easing as funds chase yield (credit sinking).

### Treasury vs. CDB Spread (国债与国开债利差)
- **Implication**: Both are risk-free, but Treasuries have a 25% tax exemption. The spread reflects this tax advantage and the better liquidity of CDB bonds.

## 2. Investment Strategies (投资策略)

### Yield Curve Strategies (收益率曲线策略)
- **Bullet Strategy (子弹策略)**: Concentrating investments in a specific maturity, usually intermediate, to balance yield and price volatility.
- **Barbell Strategy (杠铃策略)**: Investing in short and long-term bonds, avoiding intermediate. Good for volatile markets or when the curve is flattening.
- **Riding the Yield Curve (乘骑策略)**: Buying bonds with maturities slightly longer than the investment horizon and selling them before maturity to capture capital gains from sliding down a steep yield curve.

### Economic Cycle Positioning (经济周期定位)
- **Recession (衰退)**: Fundamentals down, monetary easing -> Bond Bull Market. Go long duration, prefer rate bonds or high-grade credit.
- **Overheating (过热)**: Fundamentals strong, monetary tightening -> Bond Bear Market. Shorten duration, hold cash or short-term high-grade.
- **Recovery (复苏)**: Risk appetite increases. Shift focus from rate bonds to credit bonds. Look for industry spread compression.
- **Stagflation (滞胀)**: Complex environment. Usually defensive, maintaining short duration and high liquidity.

### Liquidity Shocks (流动性冲击应对)
- If a shock is driven by demand (e.g., regulatory deleveraging) and monetary policy is tight, the bear market can be prolonged. 
- If a shock is short-term but monetary policy is fundamentally loose, the market will recover, presenting a buying opportunity for high-quality assets.
