# Huatai Fixed Income Analysis Framework (2023)

## 1. Fundamentals (基本面)
Fundamentals determine the medium-term trend of the bond market.
- **Key concept**: The core is the return on capital and financing demand.
- **Leading Indicators**: Financial data (Social Financing, M2, Credit), PMI, New housing starts.
- **Coincident Indicators**: Industrial value-added, GDP, Fixed asset investment.
- **Lagging Indicators**: CPI, PPI.
- **Structural Shifts**: Pre-2008 was CPI-driven. Post-2008 was driven by real estate/financial cycles. Recently, watch for "asset scarcity" and transitions in economic drivers (less reliance on real estate/LGFVs).

## 2. Policy (政策面)
Policy mix directly impacts the market.
- **Monetary Policy**: Watch the central bank's dual-pillar framework (Monetary Policy + Macro-prudential). Look at OMO, MLF, RRR cuts, and policy rates. The mechanism is OMO/MLF -> LPR -> Loan/Deposit rates.
- **Fiscal Policy**: Deficit rate, local government bond issuance pace.
- **Regulatory Policy**: Asset management rules, bank capital management rules.

## 3. Liquidity (资金面)
Liquidity directly determines the short-end of the yield curve.
- **Metrics to watch**: R007, DR007, OMO rates, SLF rates.
- **Logic**: Central bank controls liquidity -> impacts short-end rates -> affects yield curve steepness. 

## 4. Supply & Demand (供求面)
- **Supply**: Issuance rhythm of Government bonds (Local gov bonds are the largest category), Financial bonds, Credit bonds.
- **Demand (Institutional Behavior)**: 
  - *Banks*: Main configuration force.
  - *Wealth Management (理财)*: Amplifier of volatility (due to net-value transformation).
  - *Funds*: Trading and configuration.
  - *Foreign Capital*: Sensitive to US-China yield spread.

## 5. Sentiment & Valuation (情绪与估值)
- **Market characteristics**: The bond market has strong inertia once a trend forms.
- **Monetary-Credit Cycle**: 
  - Wide Monetary + Tight Credit = Best for bonds (Bull).
  - Tight Monetary + Wide Credit = Worst for bonds (Bear).

## Strategy Execution
- **Sources of Return**: Coupon + Capital gains (sliding down the curve + parallel shift) + Leverage.
- **Levers to pull**: Duration adjustment, Leverage ratio, Credit spread positioning.
