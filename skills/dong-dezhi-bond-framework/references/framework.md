# Dong Dezhi's Bond Trading Framework Details

## 1. Core Models

### "Double-Wheel Drive" (双轮驱动)
Driven by Economic Growth and Inflation.
- **Growth (Real Rate)**: Leading indicators include Industrial Production (IP), Fixed Asset Investment, and Exports. Acceleration implies rising capital return and financing demand, pushing up rates.
- **Inflation (Inflation Premium)**: CPI and PPI are lagging indicators. Pushes up the nominal rate.
- *Caution*: Never trade solely on CPI. If Growth (leading) turns but CPI (lagging) hasn't, follow the leading indicator.

### "Three-Factor Model" (三因素模型)
Introduces **Leverage/Financial Stability** as the third factor.
- **Indicators**: M2 vs. Nominal GDP gap, Social Financing Scale, shadow banking/wealth management size.
- **Mechanism**: When financial leverage is too high (e.g., "Financial Heat, Real Economy Cold"), the Central Bank will tighten liquidity to deleverage, causing rates to spike regardless of weak economic fundamentals.

## 2. Monetary & Credit Quadrants (货币+信用组合)

| Combination | Monetary (Liquidity) | Credit (Demand) | Bond Market Impact | Logic |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Wide Money + Tight Credit** | Loose | Contracting | **Major Bull Market** | "Asset Scarcity" - banks have cash but limited lending avenues; funds flood the bond market. |
| **Tight Money + Wide Credit** | Tight | Expanding | **Major Bear Market** | Strong financing demand meets Central Bank tightening; extreme liquidity squeeze. |
| **Wide Money + Wide Credit** | Loose | Expanding | **Bear/Steepening** | Early recovery. Policy hasn't tightened yet, but inflation expectations rise. |
| **Tight Money + Tight Credit** | Tight | Contracting | **Bull/Flattening** | Late stagflation/early recession. Cash is king initially, then transitions to a bull market. |

## 3. Timing Models

### Interest Rate Topping Model (Buy Signal)
The peak in yields often occurs after inflation peaks, during the harshest policy tightening.
1. Growth peaks, CPI still rising -> Rates oscillate at highs.
2. CPI peaks -> Central bank executes final tightening (hike/RRR increase) -> **Darkest before dawn (Optimal Buying Window for long bonds)**.

### Interest Rate Bottoming Model (Sell Signal)
The bottom in yields occurs when the economy stabilizes and monetary policy shifts.
1. Growth indicators (PMI, IP) rebound consecutively.
2. Central Bank OMO rates stop dropping or slightly rise; liquidity shifts to net withdrawal -> **Take profits and exit**.

## 4. Yield Curve & Trading Strategies

- **Bull Steepening**: Central bank cuts rates/RRR. Short-end falls faster. *Strategy: Leverage up on 3-5 year bonds.*
- **Bull Flattening**: Late-stage bull market. Long-end catches up. *Strategy: Lengthen duration, buy 10Y/30Y bonds.*
- **Bear Flattening**: Central bank tightens liquidity. Short-end spikes, long-end follows slightly. *Strategy: Cash is king, lower leverage, shorten duration.*
- **Bear Steepening**: Economic recovery confirmed, inflation expectations up. Long-end rises first. *Strategy: Short long-bonds, hold floating rate bonds.*
- **OMO Rate Anchor**: The 1-year OMO/Central Bank bill rate is the anchor. If it drops, go long. If it unexpectedly rises even slightly, it's a strong tightening signal—reduce positions immediately.

## 5. Leading Indicators & Kill-Switches

### Leading Indicators
- **M2-Social Financing Gap**: If positive and expanding -> Bond Bull.
- **OMO Rates**: If trending down -> Bull.
- **PMI**: Falling below 50 -> Recession confirmed, Bull continuation.

### Kill-Switch (Red Lines)
- **Fatal**: "Financial Heat, Real Cold" (High M2 + Weak GDP) + Central Bank expresses leverage concerns -> Immediate position reduction, switch from Long to Short.
- **Severe**: 10Y Yield at historic lows + Term spread extremely flat -> Stop chasing longs, take profits.
- **Severe**: Credit market liquidity crisis -> Reduce rate bonds temporarily to avoid liquidity shock sell-offs, buy the dip once stabilized.
- **Severe**: Central Bank shifts from loose to tight (OMO rate up + net withdrawal) -> Immediate profit-taking.
