## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Advanced Micro Devices (AMD) is the world's #2 semiconductor company by AI accelerator capability, positioned as the only credible large-scale alternative to NVIDIA for data center GPU compute. Following a decade-long transformation under CEO Lisa Su, AMD has successfully commercialized EPYC server CPUs (gaining ~35% share), Instinct AI GPUs (MI300X/MI350/MI350X), and continues to scale its Ryzen client franchise.

**Investment Thesis in Three Points:**

1. **AI GPU TAM Expansion:** The data center AI accelerator market is projected to reach $400B+ by 2028. AMD's Instinct MI300 family is gaining traction with hyperscalers and enterprises, with FY2025 Data Center GPU revenue likely exceeding $10B. Even at 15% market share vs. NVIDIA's ~80%, AMD's addressable GPU opportunity represents $60B+ annual revenue potential.

2. **EPYC Structural Share Gains:** AMD EPYC (Genoa/Bergamo/Turin) continues to take x86 server CPU share from Intel, now at ~35% unit share in key cloud segments. Turin (Zen 5) launched in late 2024 offers leading performance/watt. Server CPU replacement cycles support durable mid-teens CAGR in this segment.

3. **FCF Inflection:** FY2025 free cash flow hit $6.7B (+180% YoY), driven by operating leverage and revenue scale. With revenue growing from $22.7B (2023) to $34.6B (2025), operating margins expanded from 1.8% to 10.7%. Further scale should drive FCF margins toward 25%+ by FY2028.

**Key Risks:** NVIDIA competitive moat (CUDA ecosystem), AI CapEx cycle timing, Intel competitive response in server CPUs, elevated goodwill from Xilinx (~$25B), and valuation fully pricing execution.

## SECTION 1: COMPANY OVERVIEW

### 1.1 Business Description

Advanced Micro Devices, Inc. designs and sells x86 CPUs, discrete GPUs, adaptive processors (FPGAs), and AI accelerators. The company is a fabless semiconductor designer, outsourcing manufacturing primarily to Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company (TSMC). AMD competes directly with Intel Corporation (CPUs) and NVIDIA Corporation (data center GPUs).

Founded in 1969 and headquartered in Santa Clara, CA, AMD was largely considered a second-tier competitor until Lisa Su took the helm as CEO in 2014. Under her leadership, AMD executed an extraordinary product roadmap turnaround via the Zen CPU architecture (2017) and RDNA/CDNA GPU architectures. The 2022 acquisition of Xilinx ($49B) added FPGAs, adaptive SoCs, and software capabilities.

**Key Insight:** AMD's MI300X advantage in raw HBM memory bandwidth (which matters for large language model inference) has driven adoption for specific inference workloads. However, NVIDIA's CUDA software ecosystem creates a switching cost moat that AMD's ROCm open-source alternative has not yet overcome at scale. AMD's strategy is to be the "open alternative" and win customers at the margin of NVIDIA's supply constraints.

## SECTION 6: INVESTMENT THESIS & CATALYST CALENDAR

### 6.1 Bull Thesis

**Thesis Statement:** AMD is the only credible high-volume AI GPU alternative to NVIDIA, competing in a $100B+ and growing market with a CEO who has consistently outperformed expectations and a balance sheet capable of sustaining aggressive R&D investment.

**Supporting Pillars:**

1. **Hyperscaler Multi-Sourcing Mandate:** Major cloud providers (Microsoft Azure, Meta, Oracle) are structurally motivated to avoid NVIDIA dependence. AMD's MI300X is already deployed at multi-billion-dollar scale at MSFT and Meta for inference workloads. This is not a "pilot" — it is strategic sourcing diversification.

2. **ROCm Ecosystem Maturation:** AMD's open-source ROCm software stack is gaining traction, particularly for inference (vs. NVIDIA's CUDA dominance in training). As inference workloads grow to represent 70%+ of AI compute spend, AMD's software gap narrows as open-source frameworks (PyTorch, ONNX) increasingly abstract hardware differences.

3. **EPYC Turin on TSMC N3:** The combination of Zen 5 architecture + leading TSMC node gives AMD a structural manufacturing advantage over Intel for 2-3 years. Major hyperscalers have already publicly committed to EPYC Turin for next-generation server deployments.

4. **Fabless / TSMC Leverage:** Unlike Intel's integrated model, AMD benefits from TSMC's continuous process node leadership without carrying fab capex (~$20B+ for Intel's IFS). AMD's CapEx was only $974M in FY2025 (2.8% of revenue) vs. Intel's capex of $10B+ — this radically improves FCF generation and ROIC.

5. **Lisa Su CEO Premium:** Track record of 10-year compounding (AMD went from $2/share in 2015 to $213 in 2026) provides continued confidence in strategic execution. No material succession risk identified.
